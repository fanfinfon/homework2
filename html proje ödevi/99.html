The Petronas Towers are iconic twin skyscrapers in Kuala Lumpur, known for their impressive architecture and city views. Standing at a height of 451.9 meters, they were once the tallest twin towers in the world.

Batu Caves is a limestone hill with a series of caves and cave temples. The site is dedicated to Lord Murugan and is a popular pilgrimage site for Hindus. The giant golden statue of Lord Murugan and the colorful steps leading to the caves are highlights of the visit.

Merdeka Square, also known as Dataran Merdeka, is a historical landmark in Kuala Lumpur. It was here that the Malaysian flag was hoisted for the first time in 1957, marking the country's independence. Surrounded by colonial-era buildings, the square is a symbol of Malaysia's history.
