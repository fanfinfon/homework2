Athens

"Pearl of the Aegean"

Welcome to Athens

Athens is the capital and most populous city of Greece. The city is the fourth largest capital of Europe with a population of nearly 4 million. is located in the Attica region in the south of the country. Athens, considered the historical capital of Europe, has a long history where the first settlements were made in the Neolithic Age. In the 5th century BC, the city's values and civilization had universal importance. During this period, known as the Golden Age of Pericles, the city reached the peak of its fascinating history. Over the years, many conquerors occupied Athens and built magnificent monuments. The city, which became the capital of the modern Greek state in 1834, has become an attractive metropolis with its unrivaled charm for two centuries.

Athens is the Capital of Greece, on the coast of the Aegean Sea

How to Get to Athens?

Turkey to Athens is accessible via air, land, sea, and rail. Athens is regularly served by direct flights from Istanbul. If you choose to go by road, you have two options: either bring your own car or look into bus companies that run trips between Istanbul and Athens. You must first go from Istanbul to Thessaloniki in order to take the rail to Athens. You can take another train to the city after you arrive in Thessaloniki. The trip by train may take more than twenty-four hours. In order to facilitate maritime transportation, you must first travel to the Greek islands near Turkey. You can take a ferry to Athens after arriving to the islands of Chios, Kos, and Rhodes from locations like Marmaris, Bodrum, and Çeşme. It should be noted that shipping is a very expensive mode of transportation.

When to Go to Athens?

Athens, the oldest city in Europe, has an extensive history including stories of gods, goddesses and successful athletes. You can find many activities to do at any time of the year in the city, which promises its visitors clear and turquoise waters, clean beaches, lively nightlife, a nice shopping experience and countless ancient temples.

In Athens, which experiences the spring months from April to mid-June, the weather is warm and the crowds are less frequent.Spring and autumn are the best times to visit Athens. If you are lucky, you can enjoy the beautiful weather and clear skies without getting caught in rainy weather. The average temperature in May and October is 20 degrees. These months are ideal for visiting ancient places. If you do not like large crowds, you can visit in the first week of April, when Greek Orthodox Easter is celebrated. You can enjoy the quiet beauty of the streets during this week when many shops and restaurants are closed.

Temperatures are generally high in the city, where a dry and temperate climate prevails during the summer months. In July, the temperature can reach 38 degrees. Although Athens experiences its peak tourism during the summer months, it is one of the worst times to visit. It is worth mentioning that the traffic density in the city, whose crowds increase in summer, also increases.

Where to Stay in Athens?

There are many accommodation areas in Athens that appeal to every budget and where you can stay comfortably. In this section of our article, you can get information about certain neighborhoods and districts of Athens. Athens city center has areas with various features for safe accommodation. Plaka, the oldest district of Athens, is one of them. It has beautiful homes, plenty of restaurants and bars, and is filled with some of the city's best museums and archaeological sites. This picturesque neighborhood is the most preferred place for tourists to stay. If you are thinking of visiting Athens for a few days, you should definitely stay here. Another good areas to stay in the city are Psirriand Monastiraki. Psirri, the center of nightlife, has a very lively atmosphere with its neighbor Monastiraki. If entertainment life is an essential part of your holiday, you can choose these regions. Perfectly located, Syntagma is the central square of Athens. A modern historical centre, it is often a meeting point for people. There are many hotels in Syntagma, where you can reach touristic spots on foot. Located at the foot of Lykavittos Hill, Kolonaki is a neighborhood full of flashy, luxury and designer stores. You can stay here for a luxurious and quiet holiday experience.

Places to Visit in Athens

There are dozens of places to visit in the city center and surrounding areas of Athens that will take you on a long journey through history. The Acropolis, which has temple ruins dating back thousands of years and is the most famous tourist spot in Athens, is the Acropolis Museum, where more than 4 thousand archaeological artifacts from different periods are exhibited.Agora, the trade and administrative center of Ancient Athens, Olympieion, a two thousand year old temple dedicated to Zeus National Archaeological Museum of Athens, where more than 11 thousand artifacts are exhibited and has 5 different collections, Plaka, the most picturesque neighborhoods of Athens with wonderful views. Anafiotika, the enormous Panathenaic Stadium, with a capacity of 60 thousand people, where the first Olympic Games were held, Byzantine Museum, where you can see all kinds of artifacts and ruins from the Byzantine Period, Cyclades, where thousands of ruins belonging to the Cyclades, who ruled in the Aegean Islands 5 thousand years ago, are exhibited. The Art Museum and the Holy Apostles Church, which is the only surviving part of the Ancient Agora with a thousand-year history, are among the must-see places.
